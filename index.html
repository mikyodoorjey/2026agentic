McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte: thousands of executives surveyed on adoption rates, budget splits, pilot failures. None of it covers what is happening at the edge: where the org chart is a config file, governance is a kill switch, and the human-agent composition is the unit of work.

The frontier is producing organizational patterns that have no precedent. New economic models, new governance primitives, new ways of dividing labor between humans and machines. This research catalogs what is emerging, names the patterns that work, and builds the first taxonomy of what breaks. The study also includes enterprise leaders driving AI adoption, so the data maps agentic work from both directions.

The Research

Six open problems at the frontier.

01

Composition Anatomy

What does a working human-agent composition look like in production? Where do you draw the autonomy boundary, and how has that line moved as your operation scaled?

02

Governance Without Hierarchy

Git history as audit trail. Markdown as policy. Kill switches. Onchain registries. You built governance from scratch because nothing existed. We are cataloging which patterns hold and which are one bad decision from catastrophe.

03

The Failure Bestiary

Hallucination cascades. Recursive loops that eat compute. Agents fabricating data. Memory corruption at 3am when you are the only human in the loop. These failure modes need a taxonomy built from real incidents, not theory.

04

The New Economics

Per-seat is dying. When agents do the work, what is the unit of value? Outcome, token, task, hour of digital labor? You are running these pricing experiments live. We are capturing which ones hold.

05

The Scaling Paradox

What works at ten people does not work at a hundred. Growth forces Born Agentic firms to build the organizational complexity they were designed to avoid. We are documenting how that tension resolves.

06

The Composability Problem

MCP has 97M monthly downloads. A2A has 150+ backers. In practice, your stack is three frameworks, two protocols, a custom memory layer, and duct tape. We are mapping where composability holds in production and where it falls apart.
