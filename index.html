The consulting industry has surveyed thousands of executives about strategy. The data tells us 88% of organizations are experimenting. That enterprise spending exceeded $300 billion in 2025. That agents are being adopted at scale.

What it does not tell us: what happens inside an organization when a pilot meets production, a workflow gets redesigned, or a middle manager decides to quietly block a deployment.

That question requires primary research — structured conversations with practitioners who are in the field right now, hitting the walls, building operational knowledge that no strategy deck captures.

That is what this program produces — with your help.

The findings get published as The State of Agentic AI at Work, Q2 2026. A collaborative research report built entirely on primary data from people doing the work.

About CMPRSSN

Research that creates a category

CMPRSSN studies the coupling between people and agentic systems — the specific configuration of human-agent integration that defines how an organization actually uses AI at the institutional level, not just the individual one.

This program is the first cycle of what becomes a semi-annual research series. Primary research that creates a speaking platform, a practitioner network, and a category-defining reference that compounds over cycles.

Research led by Mikyö Clark, Lisa Rousey-Rueth, Ryan Van Sickle, and Jeremias Pedreira.
