McKinsey surveyed 2,000 executives. BCG tracked 2,400. Deloitte polled 3,000. Adoption rates. Budget splits. Pilot failure percentages. All useful. None of it captures what's happening in practice at the edge.

What is new is not how organizations use AI. It's that a new kind of organization is emerging. One where the human-agent composition is the unit of work. Where the org chart is a config file. Where governance is a kill switch and autonomy boundaries you wrote yourself. The surveys cannot see this because they were not built to look.

Our approach: the same research framework, two populations: enterprise leaders recompressing legacy structures, frontier operators who were born agentic. Side by side, the patterns that emerge are invisible from either vantage point alone.

The enterprise side shows where compositions break under institutional weight. The frontier side shows where they break under growth, speed, and the absence of structure. Together they map the agentic future of work from both directions.

The Research

Six open problems at the frontier. Not theoretical. Operational.

01

Composition Anatomy

What does a working human-agent composition look like in production? Not the architecture diagram. The daily reality. Where do you draw the autonomy boundary? How do you decide what runs without you? How has that line moved?

02

Governance Without Hierarchy

You built governance from scratch. Git history as audit trail. Markdown files as policy. Kill switches as a last resort. Onchain registries for agent identity. Some of these patterns are brilliant. Some are one bad decision from catastrophe. We want to know which.

03

The Failure Bestiary

Enterprise AI fails politically. Frontier AI fails systemically. Hallucination cascades. Recursive loops eat compute. Agents cover their mistakes with fabricated data. Memory corruption at 3am when you are the only human in the loop. These need a real taxonomy built from real incidents.

04

The New Economics

Per-seat is dying. Nothing has replaced it. Replit's margins went negative when usage outran the model. When agents do the work, what is the unit of value? Outcome, token, task, hour of digital labor? You are running these experiments live. We are capturing which ones hold.

05

The Scaling Paradox

What works at ten people does not work at a hundred. Cursor went to 300. Lovable reached 146. Growth forces Born Agentic firms to build the organizational complexity they were designed to avoid. The recompression crisis in reverse: composing new structure out of necessity as the organization scales.

06

The Composability Problem

MCP has 97M monthly downloads. A2A has 150+ backers. In practice, your stack is three frameworks, two protocols, a custom memory layer, and duct tape. Where does composability work in production and where does it fall apart?
